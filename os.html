Let's Visualise the Past



Over the past few decades, there has been a dramatic increase in the number of vehicles on the road, both in developed and developing countries. In the 1980s, there were around 500 million cars in the world, while today there are more than 1.2 billion cars on the road. This growth is driven by factors such as rising incomes, urbanization, and improvements in transportation infrastructure.
The increase in the number of vehicles has brought with it a range of environmental and social challenges. One of the most significant challenges is air pollution, as the emissions from vehicles are a major source of harmful pollutants such as nitrogen oxide and particulate matter. This pollution can have significant impacts on public health, contributing to respiratory and cardiovascular problems, and even premature death in some cases.
